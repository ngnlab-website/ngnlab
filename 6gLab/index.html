6G Ubiquitous Connectivity Lab

6G UCLab (Ubiquitous Connectivity Laboratory) is a research and innovation hub focused on advancing next-generation wireless networks beyond 5G. The lab is sponsored by Telecom Centres of Excellence (TCOE), Department of Telecommunications (DoT) under the Telecom Technology Development Fund (TTDF) initiative, reflecting a strong commitment to indigenous research and technology development in future communication systems.

Our research primarily focuses on AI-native Open RAN (O-RAN) architectures, with an emphasis on intelligent, adaptive, and scalable network design. We work on developing data-driven algorithms, xApps, and rApps for autonomous RAN control, resource optimization, and end-to-end performance assurance, enabling ubiquitous, reliable, and low-latency connectivity for emerging 6G use cases: Healthcare, Agritech and Autonomous Vehicles. We also address quantum-enabled optimization and quantum-safe security aspects for 6G networks.
