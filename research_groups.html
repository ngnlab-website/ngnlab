Research Groups

The NGNLab research groups are multidisciplinary, which include faculty and students making ground-breaking contributions in cutting-edge fields such as artificial intelligence, cyber security, computer vision, and blockchain technology to revolutionize 5G/6G, Internet of Things, Unmanned Aerial Vehicles, and Autonomous Vehicles. In collaboration with national and international institutions and corporates, the research groups conduct world-class research and innovations.

Next Generation Networks (5G/6G)
The explosive growth of 5G and the emergence of 6G have transformed wireless communication with ultra-low latency, high bandwidth, and massive device connectivity. Our research drives next-generation innovations, including 5G/6G systems for intelligent mobility and automation, and software-defined networking (SDN) for agile, programmable network control. We develop AI-driven security mechanisms for real-time threat detection and mitigation, design Open-RAN architectures with intelligent RICs, and utilize srsRAN and Open5G testbeds for prototyping and validation. We also explore blockchain-based frameworks to ensure secure and decentralized communication.
Quantum Network Security
Quantum Network Security focuses on developing cryptographic systems resilient to quantum computing threats. Our research covers Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) for secure key exchange, Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) for quantum-resistant algorithms, and quantum-safe communication protocols. We also explore cryptography standards for future interoperability, benchmark PQC-enabled TLS in real-world scenarios, and integrate quantum-safe mechanisms into 5G/6G networks to protect next-generation communication systems.
Internet of Medical Things (IoMT)
IoMT (Internet of Medical Things) combines healthcare devices and IoT technology to address the growing complexity of conditions like cancer and cardiotoxicity. Our research applies deep learning, transfer learning, and attention-based networks to analyze multimodal health data, including imaging, biomarkers, and bio-signals. Key developments include CNN models for diagnosis, multimodal fusion frameworks like TLCD-Net, and explainable AI for cardiotoxic risk stratification. Looking ahead, 5G/6G-enabled IoMT networks will enhance real-time data transmission, connectivity, and predictive analytics, advancing personalized healthcare and IoT-driven medical systems.
Unmanned Aerial Vehicles
An unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), or drone, is an aircraft that operates without an onboard pilot or passengers, either remotely piloted or autonomously. Our research advances UAV technologies across key domains, including post-disaster UAV deployment for restoring communications and supporting rescue operations, swarm UAV communication for coordinated aerial networking, and UAV-based smart farming for precision agriculture and environmental monitoring. We also explore UAV blockchain security for secure data transmission in distributed aerial systems, UAV indoor navigation for autonomous operation in GPS-denied environments, and the integration of UAVs with 5G/6G networks for ultra-reliable, low-latency communication in mission-critical applications.
Autonomous Vehicles
Autonomous vehicles (AVs) are poised to transform industries like transportation, urban planning, and insurance by enhancing efficiency, safety, and sustainability. Our research focuses on AI-Based Vehicle Security, Smart Platooning for optimized traffic flow, and Intelligent Traffic Management using AI and IoT. We also explore Cooperative Autonomous Driving for real-time vehicle collaboration and Advanced Navigation and Perception for improved accuracy. Additionally, we investigate 5G/6G-V2X integration to enable ultra-low latency communication between vehicles and infrastructure, building resilient, scalable, and intelligent AV systems for future smart cities.
